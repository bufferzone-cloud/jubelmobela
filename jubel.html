My Journey

Every Breath is a Second Chance: The Autobiography of Jubel Mobela

My name is Jubel Mobela, and this is my story—a journey shaped by struggle, lifted by hope, and driven by the belief that every breath is a second chance. From humble beginnings in Luanshya, Zambia, to the foundation of growing tech enterprises, my life has never followed a straight path.

I was born on July 21, 2002, in Luanshya, Zambia, during a time of personal and familial transition. When my parents separated at a young age, I began a life marked by movement—both physical and emotional. I first lived with my grandmother, who offered me her warmth and strength. Over the years, I moved between various relatives: from Kitwe to Lusaka, then back to Luanshya each place offering new challenges, new beginnings, and second chances to find stability.

Chapter 1: A Childhood in Motion
Though life often felt unpredictable, I learned to embrace each new environment. Change became my constant companion, and adaptability became a necessity. My early school years saw similar shifts. I began in a private school in Kitwe, only to transfer to a public school in Nkana East. After my aunt's husband passed away, I moved back and forth between families. But through it all, I remained resilient. Each move was a breath a new chance to learn, grow, and hold on to the hope that things would get better.

Chapter 2: Education as Escape and Empowerment
Education became my anchor. At Nkana Basic School in Kitwe, from Grade 5 to Grade 7, I poured my energy into academics and graduated with high marks. It was the first time I felt that my hard work could truly open doors for me. I continued my journey at Malela Secondary School, where I sat for my Grade 12 exams in 2018—and passed with excellence. School was more than a place of learning. It was where I began to imagine a future beyond struggle. Every lesson, every exam, every long walk to class was a breath a second chance to rewrite my future.

Chapter 3: Stepping Into the World
In 2019, I took my first steps into the professional world as a teacher at a private school. For two years, I taught students not just what was in their textbooks, but what I was learning myself—that you could come from little and still dream big. I learned how to communicate, lead, and inspire—skills that would shape the rest of my life. Then in 2021, I interned at Power Tools Logistics as an auditor. It was my first true exposure to the corporate world. I was now wearing formal shoes, sitting in boardrooms, handling data, and solving real business problems. It felt like I had walked through a door I once thought was closed to me. Every experience was a new breath. A second chance to discover who I could be.

Chapter 4: The Tech Path and a Turning Point
My love for technology had always been simmering beneath the surface. In 2022, I followed that passion to Northern Technical College (NORTEC) in Ndola, enrolling in a Diploma in Computer Systems Engineering. I poured everything into my studies, earning recognition as the best student year after year. For the first time, I didn't just feel like I belonged I felt like I was thriving. But life reminded me that growth often walks hand in hand with grief. Just before graduation in September 2023, I lost my father. The pain was overwhelming, and for a moment, it felt like time stood still. But in the silence that followed, I remembered that each breath was still mine to take—and that meant I had another chance to carry on, in his honor. His passing didn't end my journey. It sharpened my purpose.

Chapter 5: Building a Future from the Ground Up
In mid-2024, I joined Zamlift as an IT Technician, working hands-on with systems, networks, and digital infrastructure. It felt like everything I had learned was finally being put to use. But my ambitions didn't stop there. I launched BUFFER ZONE, a graphic design and tech company I ran as a side hustle. It was a passion project born out of creativity, curiosity, and a desire to empower small businesses with digital tools. In early 2025, I took an even bigger leap by founding Zatralo Zambia, an online platform that allows Zambians to easily purchase bus tickets. It was a solution to a problem I had seen firsthand—a digital bridge connecting people with the freedom to travel, plan, and explore.

Chapter 5½: Two New Ventures – Zynapse & Jubel
Driven by the belief that technology can transform everyday life, I expanded my vision. I founded Zynapse, a next‑generation communication app built with privacy at its core. Zynapse offers end‑to‑end encrypted messaging, high‑definition video and audio calls, and secure file sharing — a protected space for conversations. Soon after, I launched Jubel, a comprehensive ride‑share and delivery platform. Jubel provides affordable rides (JubelGo, JubelXL), parcel and food delivery, real‑time tracking, and 24/7 support. Both companies reflect my commitment to solving real problems: safe communication and seamless transport. They are my latest second chances to make a difference.

Chapter 6: Overcoming Imposter Syndrome
Even as I built companies, I often felt like an outsider in boardrooms and tech meetups. I doubted whether a boy from Luanshya could compete on a global stage. But I learned that authenticity is a superpower. I leaned into my unique perspective, and that became the foundation of my leadership style. Today, I mentor young Zambian tech enthusiasts, sharing the message that your background does not determine your ceiling.

Chapter 7: Vision for 2030
I envision a connected Africa where digital solutions are built by Africans for Africans. Zynapse and Jubel are just the beginning. I am already prototyping an AI‑driven financial literacy tool for rural communities. Every line of code, every pitch meeting, every late night is fueled by the memory of my father and the resilience of my grandmother. They taught me that every breath truly is a second chance.

Final Words
Every struggle I've faced, every loss I've endured, and every success I've celebrated has reminded me of one thing:

Every breath is a second chance.
A second chance to learn.
A second chance to grow.
A second chance to build the life you believe in.

"Start small, remain focused, and always be ready to grow."
— Jubel Mobela
